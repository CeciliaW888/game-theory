# Day 12: The Law of Eschatological Convergence
**Episode:** #12 | **Watch:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=spg58Glfz68 | **Series:** Predictive History — Prof. Jiang Xueqin

## What This Episode Is About

Prof. Jiang introduces a new concept he calls the **law of eschatological convergence**: by identifying where the end-times narratives of different religious traditions overlap, you can predict how major players in a geopolitical conflict will actually behave. This episode is the third in his series on the US–Iran war, and it explains his third prediction — that the Al-Aqsa Mosque will be destroyed during the war — through this religious lens. He argues that purely geopolitical or "realpolitik" analysis cannot explain the war; you must understand the eschatological scripts that the real players are acting out.

## Core Concepts

### Three Predictions Revisited

Prof. Jiang opens by recapping his three predictions about the US–Iran war, the first two of which he explained in Episode 11:

1. The United States will use ground troops, forcing a national draft.
2. Israel and the United States will not use tactical nuclear weapons.
3. The Al-Aqsa Mosque — the third holiest site in the Islamic world — will be destroyed during this war.

This episode focuses entirely on explaining and justifying the third prediction through eschatological analysis.

### The Universal Law of Game Theory: Mass × Energy × Coordination

Prof. Jiang introduces what he calls the **universal law of game theory**: whoever wins a game is determined by the product of three factors.

- **Mass (人数)** — the number of people on the team
- **Energy (能量)** — the level of attention, focus, motivation, and willingness to work to win
- **Coordination (协调)** — how well teammates work together; their synchronicity

He argues these are not equally weighted:

> "The most important thing in winning a game is actually coordination. In fact, I would say it's four times more important than just mass. And energy is twice as important as mass."

In a solo game (chess, ping pong), mass and coordination are irrelevant — only energy matters. In a team sport, mass is constant, so energy and coordination determine the winner. The formula explains why a small, tightly coordinated group can defeat a large, disorganized one.

### Why Narrative Is the Most Powerful Coordination Technology

Prof. Jiang traces a hierarchy of coordination mechanisms in history:

1. **A leader** — raises energy and enables coordination, but dies eventually
2. **Family / blood ties** — shared identity promotes cooperation, but internal conflicts limit it
3. **Narrative (故事 / 叙事)** — the most powerful and durable force

A narrative functions like a script. Everyone who believes it knows what role to play, so they act together without needing constant direction. This provides both high energy (believers are motivated) and high coordination (they share a common plan) simultaneously. Crucially, narrative transcends time and borders — a movement can sustain its script across centuries and across ethnic or national identities.

The most powerful form of narrative is **eschatology** — because an eschatology is a *complete* script: it tells believers what the final outcome is, what sequence of events leads there, and what each person must do. This makes it uniquely able to generate sustained, coordinated action over very long timescales.

> "Think of a story as the operating system of a society. And as such, it's a script that they will act out."

### The Law of Eschatological Convergence

Prof. Jiang's new law states: **identify the most extreme versions of different religious eschatologies, find the points where they converge, and you can predict how geopolitical events will unfold.**

Why focus on the extreme versions? Because the mainstream of any religion tends toward patience and moderation. The extreme factions — the **accelerationists (加速主义者)** — are the ones working hardest to fulfill the script. Even if they are a small minority, their intensity drives the whole religion in a certain direction:

> "Whichever eschatology is the most extreme, the most violent, will be the one that wins out."

### Six Eschatologies

**1. Zoroastrianism (琐罗亚斯德教)** — the first eschatology; rooted in the Persian Empire. The world is a battle between truth/light and the lie/darkness. Humans must live for the light. At the end of days, the god Ahura Mazda will bring a day of judgment and create paradise. Implication for the war: Iran's Zoroastrian heritage means it fights defensively and avoids being cast as the aggressor, because in its script, it is vital to die *for* the light rather than to initiate destruction.

**2. Jewish eschatology — mainstream vs. accelerationist**

*Mainstream:* The Jews were exiled from Jerusalem because they disobeyed God (worshipped false idols). Redemption requires spiritual improvement — humility, piety, love of neighbor. When Jews collectively achieve redemption, God will send the Messiah to lead them back to Jerusalem, defeat their enemies, gather the diaspora, and build the Third Temple, creating heaven on earth.

*Accelerationist / Chabad Lubavitch:* This is heresy to most Jews. The argument is: rather than waiting passively for God to send the Messiah, believers should *force* his arrival by enacting the preconditions themselves — establishing the state of Israel, returning the diaspora, defeating enemies, and building the Third Temple. This will trigger the war of Gog and Magog; when Jews are most threatened, the Messiah will come. Most Jews regard this as blasphemy. But because the accelerationists work the hardest to achieve their vision, they exert disproportionate influence.

**3. Christian Zionism — premillennial dispensationalism (前千禧年弥散主义)**

This eschatology adapts the Jewish script with one crucial change: the Jewish Messiah becomes the Antichrist (撒旦的代表). The Antichrist is a charismatic leader who rules the world from Jerusalem, then launches the war of Gog and Magog. At that moment, Jesus returns to save the Jews, and all Christians ascend to heaven in the Rapture (被提). Christian Zionists and Zionists are allies in the early phase (the "Great Racial Project"), but according to Prof. Jiang, they will eventually diverge and fight each other.

The **Freemasons** operate within a Christian Zionist context but focus on implementation. Their goal — which Prof. Jiang connects to Pax Judaica — is to turn the Antichrist's one-world government into a system: an AI surveillance state with digital ID (the "mark of the beast"), digital currency, and a headquarters in Solomon's Temple / the Third Temple in Jerusalem.

**4. Islamic eschatology (伊斯兰末世论)**

*Sunni mainstream:* When the Dajjal (Antichrist) establishes his system, Jesus will return to defeat him.

*Shia / Iranian:* The 12th Imam Mahdi will lead the Ummah (community of believers) to victory against the Dajjal. Prof. Jiang frames this as Iran's operative script in the current war.

**5. Catholic eschatology — City of God (上帝之城)**

Drawing on Saint Augustine: the Catholic Church *is* the millennium, the Messianic age, because it represents God on earth and brings peace and prosperity. Rome fell — but this was God's plan, because Rome was the "city of earth" (power, wealth) and what matters is Jerusalem, the "city of God." The Protestant Reformation and Anglo-American empire ended Catholic dominance. For the Church to achieve its eschatology, the American Empire must fall just as Rome fell.

**6. Orthodox eschatology — Third Rome prophecy (第三罗马预言)**

Moscow will become the Third Rome. The First Rome was Rome; the Second was Constantinople; the Third is Moscow — and there will be no Fourth Rome. When Moscow becomes the Third Rome, history ends and peace arrives. This requires Russia to unite the Orthodox world (currently split between Eastern and Greek Orthodox traditions), defeat Turkiye in war, and return the Greeks to Constantinople (Istanbul). Prof. Jiang links Russia's war in Ukraine not just to border security but to this Orthodox eschatological goal: destroy Europe and NATO so the Third Rome prophecy can be fulfilled.

### The 1939 Chabad Lubavitch Rescue: Eschatological Coordination in Action

To show that eschatological coordination is not just theory, Prof. Jiang recounts the rescue of the Chabad Lubavitch Rebbe, Yosef Yitzchak, from Nazi-occupied Warsaw in 1939.

The operation required coordinating: American Chabad lawyers (hired a politically connected attorney to secure US visas), Attorney General Benjamin Cohen, Supreme Court Justice Louis Brandeis (whom Prof. Jiang identifies as a Shabbatean Frankist sympathetic to Chabad), a senior State Department official (Robert Pell), a Nazi Party economist (Wohlthat, who had studied at Columbia and owed goodwill to American contacts), Admiral Wilhelm Canaris (head of German military intelligence, a skeptical Nazi who opposed SS atrocities), and Major Ernst Bloch, a Mischling officer, who physically found the rabbi hiding in Warsaw, obtained fuel coupons and military clearances, and loaded the rabbi and his family onto a first-class train filled with Nazi officials.

Prof. Jiang's point: these actors were technically on opposite sides of a world war, yet they coordinated across all those barriers to achieve a shared eschatological goal.

> "They are able to work above the bureaucracy of different nation-states in order to achieve their eschatological goal."

### Points of Convergence — and the Predictions They Generate

Prof. Jiang surveys all six eschatologies and identifies where they align. From these convergence points, he derives specific predictions for the next 2–4 years:

| Convergence Point | Prediction |
|---|---|
| Greater Israel / Pax Judaica | One-world government based in Jerusalem; AI surveillance state (Pax Judaica); tech capital (Nvidia, Oracle, Microsoft, Google) flows to Israel |
| Al-Aqsa gives way to the Third Temple | Al-Aqsa Mosque will be destroyed during the war; Third Temple built in its place |
| Rise of anti-Semitism | Forces the Jewish diaspora to return to Israel |
| War of Gog and Magog | A final war (Persia + Russia marching against Pax Judaica) follows; this Iran war is a precursor |
| US and China exit the script | Something — likely US civil war — causes the United States and China to become geopolitically irrelevant |
| Rise of Persia | Iran wins or survives the war; activates Persian identity; becomes the Middle East superpower; GCC economies collapse |
| Russia wins in Ukraine | Orthodox Third Rome prophecy advances; Europe/NATO destroyed; Greeks returned to Constantinople |

Prof. Jiang closes the loop on his third prediction: the Al-Aqsa Mosque will be destroyed because every major eschatology either requires it or is indifferent to it. None actively opposes it. That is what convergence means — shared incentive structures across traditions that would otherwise be enemies.

### Why the War Cannot Simply End

A student raises the possibility that Trump just made a strategic mistake and will declare victory and go home. Prof. Jiang acknowledges this is possible, but argues the economic logic traps the United States:

- Iran has closed the Strait of Hormuz; the GCC depends on it to export oil and import food
- If the US withdraws, Iran demands war reparations and a toll on Strait passage; GCC money that previously flowed to the US economy flows instead to Iran
- The US economy is built on GCC investment and finance; withdrawal causes economic collapse and domestic revolution
- Therefore, even if the war was a mistake, the US is better off sending troops than facing revolution at home

> "You're better off sending people to Iran than have the people in America cause a revolution."

The student's geopolitical explanation and Prof. Jiang's eschatological explanation both point to the same outcome — which Prof. Jiang treats as confirmation that the eschatological framework is capturing something real.

## In Your Own Life

Prof. Jiang's coordination formula — Mass × Energy × Coordination — applies far beyond geopolitics. Think about any project group you have been part of. A team with twice as many people but poor coordination will lose to a smaller team that operates as a unit. The formula tells you where to invest: if you are building a team, coordination is worth more than recruiting. If you are working alone, energy (focus, practice, sustained effort) is all that matters.

The deeper lesson concerns narrative. When you join an organization, a social movement, a professional community, or even a friend group, you are also joining a story about who the players are, what the rules are, and what the desired outcome is. That story shapes which actions feel natural, which sacrifices feel worth making, and who counts as a natural ally or enemy. Recognizing the narrative your group is operating inside — and asking whether it is accurate — is one of the highest-leverage things you can do. The Chabad Lubavitch rescue in 1939 worked because every participant shared a script that made the mission feel essential. What scripts are shaping your decisions right now, and are they ones you consciously chose?

## Synthesis

**Synthesis — Sun Tzu (孙子兵法) on coordination:** Sun Tzu writes that an army that moves as one body, where the soldiers in the rear rush forward as readily as those in the front, is unconquerable — not because of numbers, but because of shared will. Prof. Jiang's coordination-first formula arrives at the same principle from game theory: *shì* (势) — strategic momentum — is produced by alignment, not mass. The Chabad rescue in 1939 is a military-grade example: a tiny network, perfectly coordinated across warring nations, defeated the bureaucracy of the Nazi state.

**Synthesis — Thucydides on belief and action:** Thucydides observes in the *History of the Peloponnesian War* that what states *believe* about each other shapes events as much as what is materially true. Athens believed it could hold its empire; Sparta believed it could not survive Athens' growth; those beliefs drove the war. Prof. Jiang's eschatological convergence adds a layer: the *religious scripts* that populations carry determine what they will sacrifice and who they will coordinate with, in ways that pure power calculations miss entirely.
